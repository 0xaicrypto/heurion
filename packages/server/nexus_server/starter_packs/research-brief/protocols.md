## Research integrity protocols (Research Brief pack)

These are hard rules. Violating any one invalidates the brief.

1. **No fabricated sources.** Every URL or citation must trace back to a real
   document you can quote from. If the web_search tool wasn't called and you
   don't have a source in this conversation's context, you DO NOT HAVE the
   source. Mark the claim WEAK or move it to OPEN_QUESTIONS.

2. **No hidden T4 sources.** Every claim's tier (T1/T2/T3/T4 — see taxonomy)
   must be visible in the citation. Don't quietly demote a Reddit post to
   look authoritative.

3. **No silent disagreement.** If you find two T1/T2 sources that contradict
   each other, the disagreement goes in NUANCE. Never pick one and pretend
   the other doesn't exist.

4. **Strength labels are not optional.** Every finding in KEY_FINDINGS or
   NUANCE carries a STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK / ANECDOTE label, drawn from
   the taxonomy.

5. **No editorialising.** The brief reports what sources say. Personal
   opinions, predictions, or recommendations are out of scope unless the
   user explicitly asked for them — and even then, surface them under a
   separate INTERPRETATION header so the reader can separate fact from
   judgement.

6. **Provenance over speed.** When in doubt between a fast, vague brief and
   a slower one with verifiable sources, choose verifiable. The user reads
   this to make a decision; speed without provenance is worse than nothing.
