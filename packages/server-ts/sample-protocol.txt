PROTOCOL TITLE: A Phase II, Open-Label Study of Pembrolizumab Combined with
Chemotherapy in Treatment-Naïve Patients with Advanced Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

STUDY ID: NSCLC-2024-PEM-002
VERSION: 2.0
DATE: 2026-07-18

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. OBJECTIVES

Primary Objective:
- To evaluate the objective response rate (ORR) of pembrolizumab combined
  with platinum-based chemotherapy in treatment-naïve patients with
  advanced NSCLC.

Secondary Objectives:
- To assess progression-free survival (PFS) at 12 months
- To evaluate overall survival (OS) at 24 months
- To characterize the safety and tolerability profile
- To explore biomarker correlates (PD-L1 TPS, TMB) with clinical outcomes

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2. INCLUSION CRITERIA

Patients must meet ALL of the following:

  1. Histologically or cytologically confirmed stage IIIB/IV NSCLC
  2. Age ≥ 18 years
  3. ECOG performance status 0-1
  4. Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1
  5. No prior systemic therapy for advanced NSCLC
  6. Adequate organ function:
     - ANC ≥ 1,500/μL
     - Platelets ≥ 100,000/μL
     - Hemoglobin ≥ 9.0 g/dL
     - Creatinine clearance ≥ 60 mL/min
     - Total bilirubin ≤ 1.5 × ULN
     - AST/ALT ≤ 2.5 × ULN (≤ 5 × ULN if liver metastases)
  7. PD-L1 TPS ≥ 1% by IHC (22C3 assay)
  8. Life expectancy ≥ 3 months
  9. Signed informed consent

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3. EXCLUSION CRITERIA

  1. EGFR mutation, ALK rearrangement, or ROS1 fusion positive
  2. Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic treatment
     within the past 2 years
  3. History of interstitial lung disease or pneumonitis
  4. Active CNS metastases (treated, stable CNS metastases allowed)
  5. Prior treatment with anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, or anti-CTLA-4
  6. Active infection requiring systemic antibiotics
  7. Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  8. Known HIV, active Hepatitis B or C
  9. Major surgery within 28 days of first dose
  10. Uncontrolled hypertension (> 160/100 mmHg)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4. TREATMENT PLAN

Arm A (Experimental): Pembrolizumab + Chemotherapy
  - Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV every 3 weeks (Q3W)
  - Carboplatin AUC 5 IV Q3W for 4 cycles
  - Pemetrexed 500 mg/m² IV Q3W for 4 cycles
  - Maintenance: Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV Q3W + Pemetrexed 500 mg/m² IV Q3W

Treatment continues until:
  - Disease progression (per RECIST v1.1)
  - Unacceptable toxicity
  - Patient withdrawal of consent
  - Maximum 35 cycles of pembrolizumab (approximately 2 years)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5. SAFETY MONITORING

Adverse Event Assessment:
  - CTCAE v5.0 grading for all AEs
  - Weekly during Cycle 1, then every 3 weeks
  - DLT evaluation window: first 21 days (Cycle 1)

Dose-Limiting Toxicity (DLT) Definition:
  - Grade 4 neutropenia lasting > 7 days
  - Febrile neutropenia (ANC < 1,000/μL + fever > 38.5°C)
  - Grade 4 thrombocytopenia or Grade 3 with bleeding
  - Grade ≥ 3 non-hematologic toxicity (except nausea, vomiting,
    diarrhea controlled with supportive care)
  - Grade ≥ 2 pneumonitis
  - Any treatment-related death

Stopping Rules:
  - DLT rate exceeds 33% in first 12 patients
  - Any Grade 5 (fatal) adverse event
  - ≥ 3 Grade 4 treatment-related AEs

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6. ASSESSMENT SCHEDULE

Screening (Day -28 to -1):
  - Informed consent, medical history, physical exam, vital signs
  - ECOG PS, CBC, chemistry panel, coagulation
  - Tumor assessment (CT/MRI per RECIST v1.1)
  - PD-L1 IHC testing (archival or fresh biopsy)
  - Pregnancy test (if applicable)

Treatment Visits (Every 3 weeks):
  - Physical exam, vital signs, ECOG PS
  - CBC, chemistry panel
  - AE assessment (CTCAE v5.0)
  - Concomitant medications review

Tumor Assessments:
  - Every 9 weeks (± 7 days) for first 12 months
  - Every 12 weeks thereafter
  - Confirmation of CR/PR required ≥ 4 weeks after initial response

End of Treatment (within 30 days of last dose):
  - Physical exam, vital signs, ECOG PS
  - CBC, chemistry panel, coagulation
  - Tumor assessment
  - AE resolution assessment

Follow-up:
  - Safety follow-up: 30 days after last dose
  - Survival follow-up: every 3 months for 2 years

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

Sample Size:
  - Target enrollment: 60 patients
  - Simon's two-stage optimal design
  - Stage 1: 25 patients (≥ 7 responses → proceed to Stage 2)
  - Stage 2: 35 additional patients
  - Total: 60 patients

Primary Endpoint Analysis:
  - ORR estimated with 95% exact binomial confidence interval
  - Null hypothesis: ORR ≤ 30% (historical chemotherapy alone)
  - Alternative hypothesis: ORR ≥ 50%
  - One-sided α = 0.05, power = 80%

Secondary Endpoint Analysis:
  - PFS and OS estimated using Kaplan-Meier method
  - Median and 95% CI reported
  - Exploratory: Cox proportional hazards for biomarker subgroups
